Privacy Policy

Last updated: February 2026

AngelCare ("we", "our", "us") is committed to protecting user privacy. This Privacy Policy explains how data is collected, processed, stored, and controlled when using the AngelCare safety monitoring system.

AngelCare is designed around data minimization, user control, and security by default.

1. What Data AngelCare Processes

1.1 Video Data

  • Live video streams from user-connected cameras (e.g., RTSP / ONVIF)
  • Video frames processed in memory for safety inference
By default, AngelCare does not permanently store raw video.

1.2 Derived Safety Data

  • Detected safety events (e.g., fall, immobility, distress)
  • Event timestamps and duration
  • Risk level classifications
  • Model confidence scores

This data is stored as structured metadata (e.g., JSON), not raw video.

1.3 User-Provided Context

  • Behavioral or medical context provided by users or caregivers (e.g., mobility limitations, atypical gait, cognitive conditions)

This data is used solely to personalize inference behavior and is never shared with third parties.

1.4 Account and Contact Information

  • Caregiver contact details (email, phone number)
  • Alert preferences and notification rules

2. How AngelCare Uses Data

AngelCare processes data exclusively to:

  • Detect safety-critical situations
  • Generate alerts and notifications
  • Personalize safety monitoring behavior
  • Improve system reliability and accuracy (in aggregate)

AngelCare does not sell data, use data for advertising, or perform behavioral profiling unrelated to safety monitoring.

3. Video Processing and Storage

3.1 Local and Edge Processing

AngelCare supports local or edge-based video processing. Video frames may be analyzed in memory and discarded immediately after inference.

3.2 Optional Video Storage

If enabled by the user:

  • Video data is encrypted at rest (AES-256)
  • Video is encrypted in transit (TLS 1.3)
  • Storage duration is user-configurable

Users may:

  • Disable video storage entirely
  • Enable automatic deletion (e.g., rolling deletion after 24 hours)
  • Retain video only for specific events

4. Data Retention

Default retention behavior:

  • Raw video: not stored
  • Event metadata: retained for safety history and auditability

Users can modify retention settings at any time, including immediate deletion of stored data.

5. Data Sharing

AngelCare shares data only when explicitly configured by the user, such as:

  • Sending alerts to caregivers or emergency contacts
  • Sharing event summaries (not raw video)

AngelCare does not share data with third parties except:

  • Infrastructure providers required to operate the service
  • When legally required

6. Security Measures

AngelCare implements industry-standard security controls, including:

  • Encryption in transit and at rest
  • Role-based access control (RBAC)
  • Access logging and audit trails
  • Separation of video data and personalization context
  • Secure infrastructure and credential management

7. User Control and Rights

Users can:

  • Enable or disable video storage
  • Set automatic deletion policies
  • Control who receives alerts and what information is shared
  • Modify or remove personalization context
  • Request deletion of their data

8. Monitoring Philosophy

AngelCare is designed to monitor safety, not surveil people.

The system minimizes data retention, avoids continuous recording by default, and prioritizes semantic safety signals over raw footage. Users remain in control of how monitoring is configured and how data is handled.

9. Children's Privacy

AngelCare is not intended for use by minors without supervision. Data related to minors is handled with heightened care and minimal retention.

10. Changes to This Policy

This Privacy Policy may be updated as AngelCare evolves. Material changes will be communicated to users.

11. Contact

For privacy or data-related questions, contact: privacy@angelcare.ai
